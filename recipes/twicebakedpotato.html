Twice-baked Potato

Description

At its simplest, a twice-baked potato is creamy, cheesy mashed potatoes cradled in crisp, salty skin. That's the recipe you see here: comforting, satisfying and pretty much ideal.

Ingredients

  • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter, cubed and softened
  • 4 large russet potatoes
  • 2 tablespoons canola or other neutral oil
  • Kosher salt
  • 1/2 cup whole milk
  • 1/2 cup sour cream
  • 1/2 cup grated cheddar cheese

Steps

  1. Preheat over to 400 degrees
  2. Soften butter from fridge
  3. Clean potatoes, poke holes on them with a fork
  4. Toss potatoes in a bowel with oil and 1/2 teaspoon of salt
  5. Place potatoes in the over under a baking sheet for 1 hour
  6. Remove from the oven and let it sit until cool
  7. Slice the top third lengthwise off the potatoes
  8. Use a spoon to scoop out the flesh
  9. Add flesh to a bowl, mash the potato flesh with a fork or potato masher
  10. Add butter, milk, sour cream, cheese and sit to combine
  11. Season generously with salt and pepper
  12. Stir in the chives
  13. Add filling to potato skin
  14. Bake the potatoes for 10 mins until warmed
